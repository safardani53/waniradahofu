Leeds fans poke fun at Millwall supporter 'on a trampoline' as bizarre antics in stands go viral

 Posted on August 16, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  270 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

LEEDS fans have poked fun at a Millwall supporter's antics in the stands during their 3-0 away victory at The Den.

The grey tracksuited man was seen jumping up and down as he desperately tried to get the attention of Leeds' away supporters.

Two perspectives of the scenes have been unearthed.

From the Millwall end, the tracksuited man is seen hopping up and down with one leg on a seat to propel him up.

He was heard shouting expletives at the Leeds supporters and asking them for a fight.

The Leeds perspective shows the fans taunting him with a chant that ended in "f*** off."

In response to the man's hopping the Leeds fans then began chanting "What the f****** hell is that?"

It didn't take long for the videos to make their way to Twitter and for the memes to begin.

One Leeds fan compared the hopping to House of Pain's 1992 hit "Jump Around".

He captioned the Millwall fan jumping up and down with the opening lines of the song, saying: "Pack it up, pack it in, let me begin."
