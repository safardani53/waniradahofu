Made In Chelsea start date confirmed as series is given major shake-up

 Posted on August 5, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  349 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

MADE In Chelsea will return on Monday night for the launch of its 25th series - and there's good news for fans.

The cast are back in SW3 following a dramatic five-episode special filmed in Bali, which aired in December.

Normal business will resume with hour-long episodes set to air weekly, kicking off on Monday at 9pm.

The next episode will be available to stream immediately after on All4.

There's tension from the off as Miles Nazaire is surprised Yas Zweegers' new boyfriend Guy who she met in Bali has come back to London with her.

New parents Maeva D'Ascanio and James Taylor are back following the birth of their son Beau.

Meanwhile fans will be able to follow Harvey Armstrong in a hi-vis jacket and a hard hat at the building of his new business project.

The entrepreneur - who founded a craft beer brand called Prime Time with his former co-star Sam Holmes - is opening up a new bar on King's Road.

It's set to be Chelsea's new celebrity hot spot when it opens later this spring.

This week, Sam and Harvey celebrated the launch of Prime Time selling on draft at their local pub, The White Horse in Parsons Green.

The popular haunt, nicknamed The Sloaney Pony, was first established as a coaching inn back in 1688.

It's now a well-known hang-out spot for Made in Chelsea stars including Ollie Locke and his husband Gareth, Reza Amiri-Garroussi, Tom Dolemore and Issy Francis-Baum who were out supporting Harvey and Sam's launch on Thursday night.

Love Island star Wes Nelson was a special guest who joined punters enjoying a pint of the low-carb, low-calorie, gluten and vegan-friendly beer.

Meanwhile Made In Chelsea fans are gearing up for its return on Monday night.

In the first episode, Harvey pitches his business to his love interest Issy's dad Marc who is a successful entrepreneur and businessman, co-owning London businesses Barworks Ltd/Goodlife Group (The Diners), Camden Town Brewery and East London Liquor Co.
