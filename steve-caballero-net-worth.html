Steve Caballero | Career

When we look at Steve Caballero’s career, he has never focused on one sport only. Throughout, he has tried skateboarding to moto crossing.

Everything changed when he met Stacy Peralta. As mentioned before, he was offered a sponsorship to pursue his career. Instead, Steve used this opportunity to master the basic core of skateboarding.

Also, he was focused on the sport since Powell Peralta funded him. Finally, after one year of intense training, Steve decided to go pro in 1980.

As a result, Steve took part in the 1980 Gold Cup Contest at Upland Park. Cab went on to win the gold medal in this event. In the same year, he won the gold in Lakewood Half Pipe Contest too.

What followed next was a subsequent title wins for two decades. He secured podium finishes in Kona/Variflex Summer Nationals and Sacramento Street Style.

Not only was he winning every event he took part in, but he was trying to contribute to the American scene too. Steve is famously known for creating the ‘Caballerial’ (also known as ‘Cab’).

It is a difficult skating trick that requires an athlete to carry a fakie 360 aerial. Similarly, he is also the inventor of the frontside boardslide.

Steve was able to secure the first position at the World Championships in Munster, Germany. Furthermore, he also performed with the same energy as in the domestics. Therefore, he was able to impress everyone on his international debut.
