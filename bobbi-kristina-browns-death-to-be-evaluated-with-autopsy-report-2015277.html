Bobbi Kristina Brown's Initial Autopsy Completed, More Tests Planned

 Posted on September 24, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  446 words  |  Aldo Pusey

Update: A representative for the Fulton County Medical Examiner's Office tells Us Weekly in a statement, "The initial portion of the autopsy has been completed. The autopsy does not show an obvious underlying cause of death and no significant injuries were noted. No previously unknown medical conditions that could have contributed to death were identified."

"Additional laboratory testing will be performed," the statement continues. "Subpoenas have been issued to obtain needed documentary information. We do not plan to complete the death certificate or determine a cause and manner of death until all test results are completed and all investigative, medical, and other documentary records are received and thoroughly reviewed. We anticipate that these processes will take several weeks."

The original story continues below.

Questions left unanswered. As previously reported, Bobbi Kristina Brown died on Sunday, July 26, at the age of 22 after nearly six months of treatment. It is still unclear, however, just what led to her untimely death and the medical details surrounding it.

A representative from the Fulton County Medical Examiner's Office in Georgia told the Associated Press in a statement on Monday, July 27, that an autopsy will be performed to further evaluate the loss of life. Brown, the only child of Bobby Brown and the late Whitney Houston, was first hospitalized on Jan. 31 after being found unresponsive in a tub in her Georgia home.

The rep told the AP that the nearly six months Brown spent undergoing various treatments may "complicate" the report. She was finally moved to hospice care last month following her time in the hospital.

"Interpretation of autopsy findings and other information will also be challenging," the office told the AP. "However, an autopsy could be helpful to address questions which may arise about the cause of her unresponsiveness and eventual death."
