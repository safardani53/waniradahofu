Things to Consider When Buying Hair Products

Hair Type

Your hair type will always play a huge factor in the products you buy. Those with thicker, curly hair will need a specific type of brush like the Curls Ultimate Detangler Brush and hair dryer attachments like the diffuser that comes with the Hot Tools Pro Artist Black Gold Ionic Hair Dryer. Those with fine hair should be extra cautious with the heat from styling tools and use a heat protectant like Living Proof Restore Perfecting Spray. They might also benefit from clip-in extensions like Hidden Crown Hair Extensions Crown Clip-Ins to add extra volume when desired. 

Oily hair needs specific shampoos like Nécessaire Shampoo and can benefit from dry shampoos like Klorane Dry Shampoo with Oat Milk in between wash days. Then there’s also the issue of dryness which can cause dandruff and a need for Neutrogena T/Sal Therapeutic Shampoo and a tool to help shed excess buildup like the Leonor Greyl Massaging Scalp Brush.

Price

Depending on the product you’re interested in, prices will vary greatly. For instance, you can spend as little as $7 on a top-rated brush or as much as $459 on hair extensions that industry experts swear by. But we don’t want you to have to overspend, so all of our “best overall” picks are competitively priced within their individual category.

Function

Most of the products on our list are user-friendly from the jump for every level of expertise with a few exceptions. That said, understanding how products can interact with each other can help you choose the right ones too. For instance, a scalp massager as an application tool can help hair oil absorb faster and shampoo clean more thoroughly than just using your fingers. 

Once you’ve blow dried your hair, you can use a rotating curling iron to finish the look. “Just roll half the length of the hair on the iron, leave out the ends, and get a perfect beach wave with minimal effort,” says Julius Michael, adding that you can “smooth it out with a flat iron, creating sleek shiny hair.”

How We Tested Hair Products

We tested tons of hair products over the past year. Before each test, we conduct thorough research into each category to determine the best or most popular products we should try. We also do our best in recruiting testers of all different hair types and ages. Keep reading for a general guide of how we tested each category, either at home, in our labs, or both.

  • Shampoos: All of our shampoo testing (apart from dry shampoo) took place outside of the PEOPLE Tested Lab in our testers’ homes over the course of several weeks to several months. For dry shampoos we ran coconut oil through our strands to see how the formulas absorbed it. With traditional shampoos, we considered the desired aims — whether to reduce oil, dandruff, or hair thinning — and evaluated if they worked in those areas. We tested qualities like feel, look, leftover residue, scent, and performance for this category of testing.
  • Hair Treatments: In our tests for treatments such as hair masks or hair oils, we did real world tests using these products in testers’ daily routines. We applied the products while the hair was still wet, and then dried it, considering how the hair looked and felt before and after the product was applied.
  • Brushes: For actual brushes, our team looked at performance, considering things like hair breakage, shedding prevention, ease of use, and comfort. For scalp massagers, our testers used these tools during and after showers to apply an assortment of products to their scalp (such as oils, masks, etc.), and considered how the massager helped with soapy lathers, absorption, and more. 
  • Heat Protectant: We tested dozens of heat protectants both in the lab and over a two-week period in testers’ homes. We took into account the various conditions needed for each spray, whether intended for wet hair or dry, and then dried the hair and used a hot tool such as a straightener or curling iron. Our team also used the heat protectants on toast to see the protection at work in a more immediate visual way.
  • Hot Tools: For hair dryers, flat irons, and rotating curling irons, our team tested how well each performed for its intended purpose or style. In some cases, we used hairstylists to get the best results and then followed up with additional home testing to make sure the tools were user-friendly for all levels. We looked at heat settings, maneuverability, and performance in these product categories. 
  • Clip-in Hair Extensions: We did two tests for clip-in extensions — one in the lab led by professional hairstylists and one at home where our testers put the extensions in by themselves. We noted how the fit and comfort of each extension was, how easy they were to attach, and how they looked.
  • At-Home Laser Hair Removal: Working between 13 popular at-home laser hair removal devices we prioritized safety and ease of use. We also considered how well they removed hair, and how quickly the hair grew back.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What products improve hair quality?

    “Hair loss and damaged hair are often a concern,” says Julius Michael. “If you are a new mom and are suffering from baby breakage you’ll want to use a hair vitamin to help slow down hair loss and speed up regrowth.” He also suggests sticking to products that “are not heavy or leave build up on your hair, causing a dull coating on your beautiful crown.”

    Joseph Michael likes “leave-in detanglers as a way to treat the hair whether you’re doing heat styling or just allowing your hair to air dry.” For clients who like to air dry their hair, he recommends using a “hydrating oil like a rosemary oil.”

  • Is Olaplex actually good for your hair?

    In short, yes. Both of our expert hairstylists mentioned Olaplex by name when recommending treatments that can improve the hair’s quality. “I always recommend for my clients to do a bond builder like Olaplex once a week,” says Joseph Michael. “For any hair type you should take vitamins or use products that have hair bonders built in like K18 or Olaplex to restore damaged hair,” agrees Julius Michael.

  • What is the best way to style hair without damaging it?

    To style your hair without damaging it, it’s important to use a combination of good treatments and tools. “Make sure you’re using good heat protectants such as leave-in detanglers,” says Joseph Michael, cautioning against hot tools “that get too hot for your hair texture.” Those with fine hair should be extra careful when choosing a heat setting because this hair type is more delicate than others.

    But damage can happen with skin sensitivities as well as from preparation with the wrong cleaning agents. “If you have a sensitive scalp, you’ll want to use a natural or organic product line,” suggests Julius Michael. He also recommends shampoos and conditioners without “unwanted chemicals.”

  • Are there certain ingredients that make for a better shampoo or conditioner than others?

    Joseph Michael suggests looking for vitamin E oil and rosemary oil. “Both strengthen the hair and help with hair growth,” he says. “I like to stay away from products that have too much alcohol which can dry out the hair and make it look a little brittle.”
