Bradley James Nowell is an American musician and singer and guitarist for the ska punk band Sublime. Born and raised in Belmont Shore, Long Beach, California, Nowell developed a love for music as a child. His father took him on a trip to Jamaica as a child, which exposed him to reggae and dancehall; then he had a strong interest in rock music when he learned to play the guitar. Nowell played in various groups until founding Sublime with bassist Eric Wilson and drummer Bud Gaugh, whom he met while attending California State University in Long Beach. During its lifetime, Sublime released 40oz albums. to Freedom and Robbin 'the Hood for commercial and critical success. Throughout the group's career, Nowell struggled with an increasing heroin addiction. He eventually became conscious after giving birth to son Jakob, but relapsed and died from a heroin overdose in 1996 at a San Francisco hotel while Sublime was touring.
