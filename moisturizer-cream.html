Moisturizing your skin daily with the top moisturizer creams in 2023 in conjunction with the right skin care regimen will prevent premature aging, and keep you looking fresh-faced and spot-free.

For those who have developed spots from using new skincare products in the past, or for those who don’t know where to begin their search, choosing the right moisturizing face cream might seem like a daunting task.

However, reading this article will help you greatly narrow down your choices. In this article we will discuss some of the ingredients to look for, and how your skin type should guide you to choose your perfect product.

Things to Consider Before Purchasing a Moisturizer Cream

Natural ingredients

Aloe vera has reparative and anti-inflammatory properties. When used on wounds, such as healing spots, it can speed up the healing process and reduce the likelihood of scarring.

  • Manuka honey

If you suffer from acne, you should consider purchasing skincare products containing manuka honey. Manuka honey is antibacterial and will reduce the number of bacteria that gather in pores. This will prevent blackheads and reduce acne outbreaks. Manuka honey is also anti-inflammatory.

  • Blue-green algae

Algae is a natural moisturizer and will massively hydrate the skin. This is a great ingredient to look for if you have extremely dry skin.

Green tea is full of antioxidants and will fight against free radicals. It will also reduce the signs of aging. This is another ingredient to look for if you often get spots as it will not clog up your pores.

Super ingredients

The following ingredients are amazing for the skin and are often found in a variety of skin-care products:

  • Hyaluronic acid

Hyaluronic acid will penetrate your skin and draw water from the air, thoroughly moisturizing your skin without adding excess oil. Using this ingredient will make your skin feel more plump and glowing.

  • Active retinol

Active retinol stimulates your skin to produce more collagen. It fills out fine lines and wrinkles, as well as, reduces the appearance of sun and age spots.

  • Amino-peptides

Amino-peptides are naturally occurring in the skin. Increasing the amount by including them in cream creates younger-looking, firmer skin.

Vitamins

Many moisturizers have added vitamins to give your skin even more of a boost. The added vitamins are usually:

Vitamin will protect your skin from damage caused by free radicals. This includes UV damage and bacteria found in dust and pollution. Damage caused by free radicals is the largest cause of premature aging.

Vitamin C contains many antioxidants that will also protect your skin from damaging free radicals. Vitamin C also reduces the skin’s production of melanin, the skin darkening hormone. It will prevent the appearance of age spots.

SPF protection

A moisturizer should not replace sunscreen. However, using a moisturizer with UV protection in conjunction with sunscreen will protect your skin from premature aging and possible skin cancer.

Absorption speed

Consider how quickly the moisturizer cream will absorb into your skin. If you don’t have much time to complete your morning routine including applying makeup, you will need to ensure you purchase a moisturizer that absorbs quickly into the skin.

Skin type

Your skin type should play the largest part in which moisturizer you use. People with different levels of oil production should choose a moisturizer accordingly:

If you have oily skin, you should look for a water-based moisturizer instead of an oil-based moisturizer. Ingredients like hyaluronic acid or blue-green algae are your skin's friends because they moisturize using water absorption instead of oil. The moisturizer should be lightweight and absorb quickly.

If you have dry skin, on the other hand, you should look for a thicker moisturizer with plenty of natural oils included. Hyaluronic acid and blue-green algae are still recommended, but with plenty of oils included to lock them in.

  • Combination

If you have combination skin, you will have patches of dry and oily skin. If this is the case, you should treat each section of skin with its own product.

If you are lucky enough to have normal skin, choose a well-balanced moisturizer with a small amount of oil and plenty of vitamin content. Your skin is already fabulous and you do not want to upset its natural oil production.

Sensitive skin

You can have any of the above skin types and also have sensitive skin. However, it is most common to have sensitive and dry skin. If you have sensitive skin, choose a moisturizer with a minimal amount of ingredients, and one that is advertised as being for use with sensitive skin.

You should also avoid artificial colors and fragrances as these are known common causes of allergic reactions.

Vegan and cruelty-free

You may want to look for a product that is not tested on animals and has no animal products in its ingredients. If this is the case look for vegan and cruelty-free labels.

  • Consistency
  • Moisturizing ability
  • Amount of time feeling moisturized
  • Scent
