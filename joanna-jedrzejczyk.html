The first female fighter from Poland started practicing Muay Thai when she was 16 and later competed in national and international tournaments.

Joanna Jedrzejczyk Professional MMA & UFC Career

Early Career

On May 19, 2012, she made her professional debut against Sylwia Juskiewicz, winning via unanimous decision. That same year, she defeated Lily Kazak by submission.

Next year on June 20, 2013, she played outstandingly well and defeated Russian flyweight champion Julia Bereezikova. 

Ultimate Fighting Championships (UFC)

In July 2014, she officially agreed to play with the Ultimate Fighting Championship. Right on her first match, she had won over Juliana Lima via unanimous decision. 

Right after this fight, Joanna faced Claudia Gadelha on December 13, 2014, and she won the fight via unanimous decision.

However, the judge’s decision faces controversy as the majority of MMA media counts the bout in favor of Gadelha. 

Who did Joanna lose the belt?

After winning several fights and titles, her reign came to an end in 2017. For the sixth defense title, she lost the match against Rose Namajunas at UFC 217 at Madison Square Garden in New York on November 4, 2017. 

Again on April 7, 2018, at UFC 223, a rematch between Namajunas and Joanna, and she lost the fight via unanimous decision.
