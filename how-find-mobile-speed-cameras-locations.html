How can you find the locations of mobile speed cameras?

 Posted on August 13, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  617 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

THERE are many different types of speed cameras you may encounter on roads in the UK.

One of the 18 kinds of traffic enforcement cameras is mobile speed cameras, which are used in roaming vans and can spot dangerous drivers on the move.

How can you find the locations of mobile speed cameras?

The majority of mobile speed cameras are run by local police forces.

They tend to be found on stretches of road where there has been a history of road traffic incidents, normally over a three year or more period.

Mobile speed cameras can be found in marked or unmarked cars.

They are also sometimes manually operated by police officers using radar guns and laser guns.

There are several ways you can find the locations of mobile speed cameras.

There are a number of apps you can download that claim to be able to track speed cameras.

Some examples are Radarbot, Waze and RadarAll.

Some drivers also like to use speed camera detectors.

These are devices that you mount on your dashboard to alert you to fixed speed cameras. 

Some detectors do alert you to mobile cameras as well.

Of course, the best way to avoid having to worry about any type of speed cameras is to stick to the speed limit.

The Association of Chief Police Officers does allow a tolerance of 10 percent plus 2 mph on speeding.

Do mobile speed cameras flash?

Mobile speed cameras often do not alert drivers that they have been caught with a flash.

This is to encourage more accurate results as drivers remain unaware that they have been monitored.

Many other kinds of cameras, such as red light cameras, will use a visible flash when capturing images of vehicles breaking the rules.

Mobile speed cameras, meanwhile, use specialised sensors or infrared technology.

How long does it take to receive a ticket from a mobile speed camera?

It can take up to 14 days for a ticket from a mobile speed camera to come through.

If your car has been caught speeding, you will be notified via post.

A Notice of Intended Prosecution (NIP) and a Section 172 will be posted to the vehicle's registered address.

Once that has arrived, you have 28 days to fill out the Section 172 Notice and send it back.

In the Section 172 you must tell the police who was driving the car.

You will then be sent a Fixed Penalty Notice within 14 days.

According to the government website, you may have to go to court if you ignore the notice.

Do mobile speed cameras work in both directions?

Yes, mobile speed cameras are designed to cover both lanes of traffic.

The camera can only capture one car at a time but the technology is so fast that both directions can be covered at once.
